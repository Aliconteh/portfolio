Alimamy Lewis Seray Conteh

Mechanical Engineer & STEM Ambassador

Alimamy Lewis Seray Conteh holds a bachelor's degree (Honours) in mechanical engineering from Fourah Bay College, University of Sierra Leone. He is the co-founder of the STEAD Society, the Sierra Leone National Point of Contact for the Space Generation Advisory Council (SGAC), and the National Outreach Coordinator for Sierra Leone at the International Astronomical Union Office of Astronomy Outreach.

Space in Africa has named him one of the Top 10 Under 30 innovators in the Africa Space Industry for 2021. He's also a recipient of the Africa Space Leader Award and the African Region's National Point of Contact for the Year 2021.
Conteh received a Youth Scholarship from the African Union Commission to attend the 8th African Leadership Conference on Space Science and Technology in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia. He was a part of the 4th African Space Generation Workshop's PR and Communications team in Ghana. He's also on the organizing committee for the 19th Space Generation Congress in Dubai. Alimamy is a STEM ambassador who is spreading the word and developing initiatives about space science and technology across Africa.

World Space Week

For the first time, the World Space Week was held in Sierra Leone, and Alimamy L.S Conteh served as the event manager. This event gathered together academics, students, and young professionals in the fields of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics to share information and discuss ideas on how we can all work together to advance the STEM discipline, specifically Space Science and Technology.

Robotics and STEM Conference

With the sponsorship of the Abu Kamara Entrepreneurship Fund, Africa Girls Empathy partnered up with STEAD Society for a two-day Robotics Competition and STEM Conference. This two-day event, which included a robotics competition on the first day and a STEM conference on the second, was designed to pique the interest of children, young professionals, and teachers in the fields of science,technology, engineering, and mathematics.

The event took place on Thursday, December 9th, 2021, and Friday, December 10th, 2021. "Rethinking STEM to support innovation for socio-economic growth" was the topic of this Robotics Competition and STEM Conference. The 300 attendees from Sierra Leone's various communities engaged with fellow students and learned from stakeholders who are shaping the STEM field during the event.
